J.K. Rowling

Biography

Joanne "Jo" Rowling, born in Yate, United Kingdom, July 31, 1965, better known as J. K. Rowling, is the British author of the fantasy story of Harry Potter, the orphan boy wizard. She was born in Chipping Sodburry, South Gloucestershire. Her publisher, Bloomsbury, wanted her to use her initials on the cover of the Harry Potter books, indicating that a female name on the cover would attract less interest from boys, so she decided to use her paternal grandmother's name "Kathleen". In February 2004, Forbes magazine indicated that Rowling's fortune amounted to 576 million pounds sterling, making her the first person to become a millionaire by writing books. She is the richest woman in the United Kingdom, and much richer than Queen Elizabeth II. The writer won the Prince of Asturias Award for Concord in 2003. Fans of the Harry Potter series are called PotterHeads. Rowling has written The Tales of Beedle the Bard, Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them and Quidditch Through the Ages, which complement the Harry Potter series. Initially, only 7 hand-made copies of The Tales of Beedle the Bard were made, 6 were sold and one was auctioned off, all the money was donated to charity, but later Rowling decided to publish it in order to donate the money raised from all the copies sold to charity, this project was carried out with the Baroness of England.
